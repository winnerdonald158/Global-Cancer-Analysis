                                                                    Global-Cancer-Analysis
                                  Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing global cancer data to understand where preventable deaths are occurring and how healthcare coverage impacts survival outcomes.

Using SQL, and Power BI, I explored patterns in cancer cases, deaths, and prevention efforts across countries, regions, and income groups.

The goal of this project is :
i. To Identify countries at high risk
ii. To Understand the impact of healthcare coverage
iii. To  Highlight opportunities to reduce preventable deaths

File 1: Raw Data
Original dataset containing cancer cases, deaths, and coverage

 Key Findings;
Total cancer cases exceed 19M+, showing that there is a need for global health challenge

Low-income countries show higher preventable deaths;
This is not due to higher income, but due to lower healthcare access and coverage

Coverage Impact;
Countries with low coverage experience higher deaths and lower prevention rates,increasing coverage directly improves survival outcomes

High-Risk Countries;
Some countries have high deaths and low coverage, making them priority areas for intervention

Regional Insights;
Certain regions show strong improvement over time while others still left behind due to healthcare limitations

Business Recommendations
1. Improve Healthcare Coverage;
Governments and organizations should invest in expanding healthcare access, especially in low-income countries

2. Focus on High-Risk Countries;
Resources should be directed to countries with high deaths and low coverage

3. Increase Preventive Programs;
Early detection and screening programs can significantly reduce deaths

4. Regions with low improvement trends need targeted interventions

5.Healthcare policies should be guided by data to maximize impact

 Key Takeaway
This project shows that many cancer deaths are preventable, but gaps in healthcare coverage continue to put millions of lives at risk.
